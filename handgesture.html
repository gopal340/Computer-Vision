Abstract

Hand Gesture Recognition (HGR) stands at the forefront of Human-Computer Interaction (HCI). This project explores a Computer Vision-based approach utilizing the Leap Motion Controller (LMC) to capture stereo infrared imagery. Unlike skeletal tracking methods that fail during self-occlusion, this study implements a High-Capacity CNN that processes raw infrared frames directly, achieving >99% accuracy.

1. Introduction

The evolution of HCI is moving away from physical contact devices towards touchless interfaces. The Leap Motion Controller offers a unique advantage by using stereo infrared cameras to track hand movements with sub-millimeter accuracy.
